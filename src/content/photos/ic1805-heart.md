A vast emission nebula in Cassiopeia, about 7,500 light years away and spanning 165 light years across. The dual-narrowband filter cuts through Glasgow's light pollution and pulls out the H-alpha and OIII signal — what looks like a featureless patch of sky to the eye becomes structure across hours of integration.
