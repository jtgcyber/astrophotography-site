A pair of interacting galaxies in Ursa Major, separated by about 150,000 light-years and gravitationally tugging at each other. M81 (Bode's) is a textbook spiral; M82 (Cigar) is a starburst galaxy with violent outflows visible as red H-alpha plumes when given enough integration time.

This was shot over three nights from a darker site than I usually get to use. The longer integration really pulled out the faint extended structure around M81 — there's a tidal bridge of stars between the two that's almost invisible in shorter exposures.
